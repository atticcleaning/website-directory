Design Directions

6 layout and density explorations within the established visual foundation. Same colors, fonts, and spacing tokens — different arrangements.

A: Clean Stack

RECOMMENDED

Single-column card stack. Full-width cards, generous spacing. The "calm competence" baseline — maximum readability, minimum visual noise. Best for Maria's anxious mobile scan.

B: Dense Grid

DESKTOP VARIANT

Two-column card grid with filter toolbar. Higher information density, compact cards. Shows more results above the fold. Filter chips and sort inline above results.

C: Editorial

CONTENT PATH

Narrow reading column, larger typography, generous vertical rhythm. Cards feel like editorial entries. Best for David's research mindset — authority over efficiency.

D: Split Panel

POWER USER

Sidebar filters + main results area. Desktop-optimized layout with persistent filter controls. More structured for users who want to narrow results aggressively.

E: Hero-Forward

BRAND PRESENCE

Dark hero section with prominent search, city quick-links strip, then results. Strong first impression, clear brand moment. Phone/website as button CTAs on cards.

F: Homepage Layout

HOMEPAGE ONLY

Complete homepage with hero search, featured city grid, and educational article highlights. Shows the full homepage content hierarchy before any search.
